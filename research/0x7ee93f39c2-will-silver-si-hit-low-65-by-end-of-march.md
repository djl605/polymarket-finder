B. CME settlement mechanics and potential for outlier settlement
- COMEX/SI active-month settlement is determined by CME staff using a defined settlement period and methodology: for Silver, the exchange computes the settlement using the volume-weighted average price (VWAP) of outright trades executed on CME Globex during the defined settlement minute (for silver that minute is 1:24:00–1:25:00 p.m. ET). If there are no outright trades, the staff will use the best bid/offer or implied spread prices closest to the last trade. That means the settlement is anchored to executed transactions during a narrow, one-minute window or, if none, to quotes/implied values. (SER-5147 / CME advisory). ([cmegroup.com](https://www.cmegroup.com/tools-information/lookups/advisories/market-regulation/SER-5147.html))
- Trading-at-Settlement (TAS) mechanics and limits: TAS is permitted in active contract months; TAS transactions may be executed at the current day’s settlement price or within a small allowed differential (e.g., +/- up to 10 ticks depending on product), so TAS can be used to transact near settlement but is constrained. The TAS rules and the fact that settlement is based on a narrow time window mean that if trading is thin in the settlement minute, a small number of trades (or a single large trade) could materially influence the VWAP settlement. Conversely, when that minute is actively traded, VWAP ties settlement closely to the prevailing market. ([cmegroup.com](https://www.cmegroup.com/education/articles-and-reports/trade-at-settlement-for-metals.html))
Implication: The settlement mechanism introduces a non-zero chance that a thin/minimally-liquid settlement minute could deliver an outlier settlement price (either up or down), meaning the market must price both fundamental moves and technical/operational settlement risk.
