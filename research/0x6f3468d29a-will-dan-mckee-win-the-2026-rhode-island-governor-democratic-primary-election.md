But the market is clearly **not very reliable as a measure of consensus**, because it has **$0 total volume**. That means:

So the market’s low price should not be treated as strong evidence by itself. However, the available public information **does point in the same direction** as the market: McKee appears to be a clear underdog.

The key question is whether he is a **15% underdog** or perhaps something more like **20-30%**. Given incumbency and the very large undecided share, I think that is the real debate. The public evidence does **not** support making him the favorite, but it may support a somewhat higher probability than the market currently implies.
