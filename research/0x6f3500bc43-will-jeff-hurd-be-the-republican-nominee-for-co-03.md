- **Hurd has the normal incumbent/fundraising advantage**, which by itself might make him a much clearer favorite.
- **Trump’s endorsement reversal creates genuine primary risk**, especially in a Republican primary where an insurgent “betrayed MAGA” narrative can matter more than general-election viability.

So 54% could reflect “Hurd still slightly favored, but barely.” That is not irrational.

However, this market has only **$1,174 total volume**, which is extremely low. That means the price may not represent deep consensus; it may reflect a few traders reacting to a headline. In a low-volume market, a number like 54% can be more of a placeholder than a robust probability estimate. The tight spread suggests someone is willing to quote around that level, but not necessarily that many informed participants have stress-tested it.

My read is that the low volume more likely reflects **limited attention** than an “obvious outcome.” If the outcome were truly obvious, the market would probably sit much further from 50 despite low volume.

But Trump’s recent endorsement switch to Scheppelman is exactly the sort of information that could compress Hurd’s odds toward a coin flip, especially this far ahead of the June 30 primary. If forced to lean, I think the evidence modestly favors **Hurd being somewhat more likely than 54%**, because:

But that edge looks **small**, not dramatic. The endorsement shock is recent, and we are still months from the June 30, 2026 primary. A lot can change once filing finalizes, caucus/assembly dynamics play out, and updated fundraising reports appear.

So the best objective conclusion is: **some mild evidence of undervaluing Hurd, but not enough to call this a clear mispricing**.
