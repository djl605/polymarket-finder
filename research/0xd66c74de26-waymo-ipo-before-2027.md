### Full Analysis

1. **CONTEXT ANALYSIS**

This market asks a narrow question: **will Waymo complete an IPO by December 31, 2026, 11:59 PM ET?**  
For a **YES**, Waymo must actually complete its first public listing on a recognized exchange before that deadline; mere rumors, confidential filing chatter, fundraising, or a spinout without a public listing would not qualify. For a **NO**, any of the following would suffice: no IPO by the deadline, acquisition/merger before then, or Waymo ceasing to exist under the market rules.

A key nuance is that **Waymo is already a separately capitalized subsidiary with outside investors, but still majority-owned by Alphabet**. That means there is a plausible path to IPO, but the market is not asking whether Waymo is valuable or whether investors want exposure. It is asking whether Alphabet will choose to undertake a full public offering within roughly 9.5 months from the current date of **March 11, 2026**. ([sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204426000018/goog-20251231.htm))

4. **MISPRICING ASSESSMENT**

On balance, I do **not** see strong credible evidence of a major mispricing.

The bullish case is straightforward: Waymo is scaling fast, has elite outside backers, an enormous private valuation, real commercialization, and clear eventual IPO potential. If someone priced this at, say, 15%, I would be more inclined to think they were underweighting the chance of a strategic spinout.

But the bearish case is stronger on the specific **before-2027** timing question. The same February 2026 financing event that makes IPO speculation feel more real also shows that **Alphabet is still the financial backstop and majority owner**. That is crucial. A company that can raise a giant private round with parent support has less need to rush into public markets within the same year. And I found **no public filing, no announced spinout, and no official management language suggesting 2026 IPO preparation**. ([sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204426000018/goog-20251231.htm))

So the evidence supports something like:
- **Not impossible**
- **Materially plausible**
- **But still more likely no than yes**

That is basically what a 26% market implies.

If anything, there may be a **small** argument that the market is a bit low because Waymo’s February 2026 financing and valuation step-change may not be fully reflected in an illiquid market. But I do not think the evidence supports claiming a large edge. The most charitable interpretation of the 26% price is that traders understand Waymo’s strength yet also understand that **“great company” is not the same as “IPO this calendar year.”**
