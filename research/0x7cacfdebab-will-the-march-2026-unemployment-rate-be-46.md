The relevant number is the one-decimal **official unemployment rate** in **Table A-15**, and **only the first release counts**. ([bls.gov](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/empsit_03062026.htm))

So:

- **YES** if BLS reports **4.6%** for **March 2026**

That makes this a question not about whether unemployment is “around” 4.6%, but whether it lands on **exactly one tenth**. For a continuous-ish macro variable with monthly noise, exact-hit markets often deserve materially lower probabilities than “is 4.6 plausible?” because many adjacent outcomes are live.
