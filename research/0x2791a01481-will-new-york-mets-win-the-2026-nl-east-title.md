The awkward part is the market microstructure: **volume is $0**. That means the quoted price may not represent broad consensus at all; it could simply be an initial listing or a small number of passive orders. So the price is **not very trustworthy as a discovery mechanism**. But low volume cuts both ways: it means inefficiency is possible, yet it also means we should be careful not to over-interpret a tiny numerical discrepancy as a tradeable edge.

Given the available evidence, the low volume looks more like **lack of engagement in a far-out preseason futures market** than proof that traders are missing major information. If informed traders arrived later, they would probably move this market only modestly unless team news changes materially.

Could the Mets be somewhat undervalued? Yes, modestly. The best bullish case is that the market is slightly underweighting the magnitude of the Mets’ offseason improvements and slightly overweighting Braves/Phillies brand priors. But the counterevidence is substantial: Atlanta’s projection remains nearly identical to New York’s, the Phillies retain a real path, and Lindor’s health adds some near-term uncertainty.
