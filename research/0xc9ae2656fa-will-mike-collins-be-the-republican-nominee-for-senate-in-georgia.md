A charitable interpretation of **84.5%** is that traders think:

2. Dooley and Carter split the anti-Collins vote;
3. Collins is best positioned for a future **Trump endorsement** or pro-Trump consolidation;
4. the low volume reflects an “obvious frontrunner” market rather than neglect.

That interpretation is possible. Collins does lead every public primary snapshot I found, and he has grassroots/MAGA strength plus some institutional backing. ([emersoncollegepolling.com](https://emersoncollegepolling.com/georgia-2026-poll-senator-ossoff-starts-re-election-near-50-and-outpaces-gop-field/))

But the market’s **$3,310 total volume** is very low for a future statewide nomination contest. With low volume, a tight spread does **not** necessarily mean efficient price discovery; it can simply mean one or two traders posted confident quotes and nobody bothered to challenge them. Given the public evidence, this looks like a market where the price may reflect a thinly traded consensus or even a single strong opinion more than a deeply informed equilibrium.

The low volume is especially important because this race is **not obviously settled**. Public polling still shows huge undecided shares, multiple credible candidates, and uncertainty around elite endorsements and possible runoff dynamics. That profile usually argues for caution in interpreting a high probability as reliable.

4. **MISPRICING ASSESSMENT**

My best read is that there is **credible evidence the market is somewhat overpriced on Collins**, though not enough to claim an extreme or near-certain error.

Why overpriced?
- Current public polling supports **“leader”** more than **“84.5% favorite.”**
- There are still multiple viable opponents.
- A runoff is plausible if nobody clears 50% on May 19.
- Trump has **not** publicly endorsed in this Senate primary, and that unresolved variable matters a lot.
- Kemp’s backing of Dooley gives the field more structure than a simple Collins march to the nomination. ([emersoncollegepolling.com](https://emersoncollegepolling.com/georgia-2026-poll-senator-ossoff-starts-re-election-near-50-and-outpaces-gop-field/))

Why not call it a massive mispricing?
- Collins is indeed the polling frontrunner.
- He may be the candidate best matched to the current Georgia GOP electorate.
- Traders may be pricing latent information not fully captured by topline polls: activist energy, expected Trump alignment, superior runoff positioning, or weak ceilings for Carter/Dooley. Those are plausible reasons informed traders could land above 50%, perhaps even materially above it. ([emersoncollegepolling.com](https://emersoncollegepolling.com/georgia-2026-poll-senator-ossoff-starts-re-election-near-50-and-outpaces-gop-field/))

Still, moving from “plurality leader around 30–32% with 33–40% undecided” to **84.5%** requires a lot of assumptions. I do not see public evidence strong enough to justify that jump. A more defensible range from the available evidence seems something like **55%–70%**, depending on how much weight one gives to Collins’ Trump-world strength and runoff prospects. That suggests a likely overpricing on the order of roughly **15–25 cents**, with the caveat that low-liquidity markets can stay wrong and that endorsement news could quickly change the picture.
