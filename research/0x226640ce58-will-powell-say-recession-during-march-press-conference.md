### Full Analysis

1. **Context analysis**

This market is narrowly about **whether Jerome Powell literally says the word “recession”** at the **March 18, 2026 FOMC press conference**, including both his opening statement and Q&A. It does **not** require him to predict a recession, endorse one, or discuss it at length; any qualifying utterance of the exact word counts. That makes the key forecasting question less “Will Powell think a recession is likely?” and more “How likely is it that either his prepared remarks or a reporter’s question prompts him to say the word out loud?”

The resolution bar for **YES** is therefore fairly low. If a reporter asks, “Are you worried about recession risk?” and Powell answers using the word, that is enough. **NO** requires that the entire press conference pass without Powell saying “recession” once.

In the **March 19, 2025** FOMC press conference, after being asked directly about recession risk, Powell said the word multiple times and gave a structured answer: he said there is always some probability of a recession, cited something like a one-in-four unconditional chance over 12 months, and said outside forecasters had raised recession odds somewhat but that it was “not high.” That shows two things: first, reporters do ask him recession questions when macro uncertainty rises; second, Powell is willing to answer directly using the exact word rather than avoiding it. This is highly relevant because the 2026 market resolves on literal wording, not underlying economic reality.
