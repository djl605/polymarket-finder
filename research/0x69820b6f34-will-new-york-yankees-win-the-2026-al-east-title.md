Low volume means the price is **less reliable**, but low reliability does not itself imply exploitable mispricing. It just means we should demand stronger external evidence before calling it wrong.

The tight spread is mildly reassuring, but with no volume it may not reflect informed two-sided engagement. It could just mean a market maker or single participant is willing to quote tightly near a generic consensus price. So I would treat this market as **weakly informative** rather than as a robust aggregator of private information.
